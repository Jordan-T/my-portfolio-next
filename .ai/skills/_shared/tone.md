# Tone conventions — single source of truth

Single source of truth for the writing tone of all French-language content on
the site (articles, pages, descriptions). The `article-writing` and
`page-content-review` skills read this file before any drafting or review.

## Posture

- Measured, analytical, factual tone. Explain a reasoning and a set of choices
  ("here's why I went with this"), never oversell.
- Honesty first: acknowledging trade-offs and what another option would have
  brought makes the writing credible. Never claim a choice wins on every
  criterion.
- The author's authentic voice, not a "polished" or "AI" style. Prefer shorter
  sentences.

## Banned self-promotion

Forbidden phrasing, because it oversells instead of demonstrating (French
originals kept, since they are the actual banned strings to grep for in
French content): « démonstration de savoir-faire », « validation
scientifique », « la preuve par les chiffres », « révélateur d'architecture »,
« je suis le meilleur », and any variant in the same register.

Use the concrete instead: what was done, why, and what it cost.

## Form rules

- **Absolute ban on the em dash (—).** The site is French: use commas, colons,
  parentheses, or periods instead. Non-negotiable rule, to be checked
  explicitly at the end of every review pass.
- No excessive bold or bullet lists. Prose carries the argument; lists are for
  when they genuinely clarify. A bullet is at least one full, useful sentence,
  never an isolated word.
- Bullet points are a structural strength: write short connecting sentences
  (the "so what") between points rather than dense blocks.

## Facts and figures

- Never invent figures or technical facts. Ask the author for the real
  measurements (Lighthouse, Core Web Vitals, GTmetrix, bundle sizes…) rather
  than estimating them.
